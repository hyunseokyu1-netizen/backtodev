When I look at people who write, I see a lot of bland book titles at first.

However, as they publish more and more books, and as publishers write titles to increase sales, they definitely become more memorable.

And books with such titles also become quite famous.


The titles of books seem to reflect the zeitgeist of the time.

Even if the title means the same thing, it seems that the title changes according to the sentence and grammar order that people of that era like.


>So, if you go to the new book section of the library and look at the titles of the books on display, you can see that this is ```the current trend```,

and you can feel that this is what people are interested in.

I don't think I've finished a book in the past year. I go to the library every week and borrow it, but I borrow it because I see the title and want to see it,
I end up carrying it around for a week, and then when I get a text to return it, I go and return it, and then I borrow it again.

I go to the library to look at the cover of a book.
